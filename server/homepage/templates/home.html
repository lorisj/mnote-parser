About me:

Hi! I am a undergraduate student studying computer science and math at the University of Michigan. I am mostly interested in problems relating to algebraic structure. I am particularly interested in problems that arise in quantum information theory, but also in other fields including foundations (math), complexity theory, and machine learning. Below I have listed some of my relevant research and work experience. Check out my project page for some of my public work.

Research Experience:

Quantum Information Theory (May 2022 - present)

I am currently working on research in quantum information theory with Prof. S. Sandeep Pradhan. So far, we have developed a way to perform quantum source coding in polynomial time, and currently we are working on some problems in network quantum information theory.

Reinforcement Learning (May 2023 - present)

I am currently working on research in reinforcement learning with Prof. Necmiye Ozay under the SURE program. We are looking into different methods for state space abstractions and how they can be used in reinforcement learning.

Work Experience:

Instructional assistant - EECS 498:Quantum Programming (Fall 2022)

I was a instructional assistant for EECS 498. I led a lab section with over 30 students, teaching them concepts in quantum computing, linear algebra, and quantum error correction.

Grader - EECS 598:Quantum Information, Probability, and Computation (Spring 2022)

I graded homeworks and answered questions on Piazza covering topics ranging from quantum error correction, probability, and algorithms.

Electrical Engineering Intern - Radiological Health Engineering Lab (Oct 2020 - May 2022)

I worked with Prof. Kim Kearfott on circuit design for a do-it-yourself smart geiger counter. I mostly worked on designing the power system, but also worked a bit on the signal processing and software design.
